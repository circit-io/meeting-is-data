What Didn't Work (and What We Changed)

Topic explosion: 373 topics / 369 meetings. 224 orphans (60%). Only 37 recur 6+ times.
Fix: restricted tags, taxonomy injection, max 3. Next: similarity gating.
Relevance noise: Standups, social calls, 1-on-1s cluttering results.
Fix: exclusion rules + sensitivity labels.
Dashboard fatigue: Another tool to check.
Fix: push into workflow via email alerts, not a new destination.
Cost model: 4 LLM calls per page load. €13–31/mo OpenAI. €125–150/mo total infra.
LLM is a small fraction of the total bill at our scale.

"Will People Be Afraid to Speak?"

Layer 1: Pre-Pipeline Gatekeeping STRONGEST
MS Purview sensitivity labels • 1-on-1 / HR / legal exclusion • Teams consent notification
Layer 2: Post-Meeting Consent AUTOMATED
Automated email to meeting owner: "Can this meeting be shared?" No consent → no publishing.
Layer 3: PII Controls WEAKEST
System prompt: no names, no compensation • Azure OpenAI PII output filter • Planned: Presidio pre-LLM
Layer 4: Governance
No audio stored • Retention policies on raw transcripts • GDPR right to erasure via participant mapping
Trust: People still verify LLM summaries — source meeting links on every panel.

No single layer is reliable alone. That's why there are four.

The Pattern (Not the Tools)

📡

The data already exists

Meetings, Slack, tickets, docs. Terabytes of org context. Nobody is routing it.

🛠

Build small, ship fast

Two weeks. ADF + OpenAI + Grafana. Side project, not a 6-month product initiative.

🔧

Make it self-service

One unified view → anyone builds their own tools. No bottleneck.

This pattern applies to any unstructured org data: incident reports, design docs, customer calls, support tickets.

Three Things You Can Do Monday Morning

  • Count your meetings last month.
    Open Viva Insights or your calendar. If the number surprises you, you have the problem.
  • Ask one lead: "What did you miss from another team?"
    If they answer immediately, you have your first topic to track.
  • List 10 topics your team cares about. Compare to reality.
    The gap between "what we care about" and "what we discuss" is your starting point.
